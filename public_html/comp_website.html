Hey, my name is Dua,
And I'm currently trying to learn Mandarin!

When I was five years old, my parents signed me up for Mandarin classes, in the hopes that I would become a fluent speaker. Their reasoning for such actions? - "Mandarin is the language of the future". The only problem - the teachers mainly spoke in Mandarin. Looking back, I think that it was more of a reading and writing school - a school meant to teach already fluent Mandarin-speaking children how to read and write the actual Chinese characters themselves. I went there, almost every Sunday, for 7 years. You must be thinking 'Oh, you must have learned a lot then!' HA HA, NOPE! Honestly, I think the only good thing I got out of going to those lessons were the cookies from the bakery nearby. I remember hating them, and having a strong hatred the Chinese language itself.
